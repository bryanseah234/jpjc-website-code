Enrichment

1. Museum Based Learning at National Gallery Singapore


As part of our curriculum, students embark on a Museum Based Learning module to the National Gallery Singapore. Past exhibitions our students have gone to include Colours Of Impressionism: Masterpieces From The Musée D’orsay and Reframing Modernism, specifically focusing on artworks from artists that are in the syllabus. The MBL module allows for students to engage with artworks in a multi-sensory manner, making space for deeper, authentic learning to happen.

2. PARK(ing) Day


PARK(ing) Day is an annual event jointly organised by SUTD and URA. On this day, people come together to creatively re-imagine regular parking lots as art spaces, injecting vibrancy and fun into the streets. Through this project, the students experience urban design, real-world prototyping, community engagement and creative expression while at the same time learn to appreciate urban culture and sustainability.

Our students conceptualised and created Build-a-Tama, an Interactive Artwork for PARK(ing) Day where the public gets to build
their own cities out of egg cartons

3. ArtLab for Secondary School Students


JPJC ArtLab is an annual Art Workshop planned and run by J2 art students for Secondary School students. Since 2017, it has served as an outreach to Secondary School students who may be interested in taking art as an ‘A’ Level Subject. It also serves as a platform for H2 Art students to take up leadership roles and explore new creative skills.

Some of our students visiting Singapore Art Week for inspiration!
